For this task, I used Canva to design a one-page infographic on digital 
literacy awareness. My infographic covered three main topics: the 
meaning of digital literacy, safe internet practices, and professional 
online presence. I selected Canva because it provides ready-made 
templates, icons, and layouts that make visual communication easier 
for beginners. The design included simple icons, short explanations, 
and color-coded sections so that students could quickly understand 
the concepts. 
One interesting thing I observed while making the infographic was 
how strongly design influences understanding. A well-organized visual 
made the information look more engaging and easier to remember. At 
the same time, I realized that using ready-made elements without 
understanding design principles can create only surface-level quality. 
The most difficult part was choosing the right balance between 
attractive visuals and meaningful content. This task helped me 
understand that digital literacy is not just about presenting information 
but also about ensuring that the message is clear, accurate, and 
responsible.
