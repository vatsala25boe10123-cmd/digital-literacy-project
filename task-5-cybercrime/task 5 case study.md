**Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

UPI and online payment fraud is one of the most common cyber crimes affecting students and working professionals in India. It usually happens when fraudsters trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fake payment requests.

For example, a college student receives a call from someone pretending to be a bank representative. The caller claims that the student’s bank account or UPI ID will be blocked unless they verify it immediately. To make the scam believable, the fraudster creates urgency and sends a payment request link through a UPI app.

The student, without checking carefully, clicks the link and enters their UPI PIN, believing it is for verification. Within seconds, money gets debited from their account. In some cases, scammers also send fake QR codes, where scanning and entering the PIN directly transfers money.

Students are usually targeted because they frequently use UPI apps for food, travel, shopping, and peer payments. The consequences include financial loss, panic, and possible misuse of bank details.

This type of fraud shows how small mistakes, such as trusting urgency or not verifying the source, can lead to major financial loss. Reporting the fraud quickly on cybercrime.gov.in or helpline 1930 can improve the chances of blocking the transaction.
